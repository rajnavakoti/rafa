# Developer Portal

## Introduction

Developer Portal aims to provide a high quality developer experience for Engineers in accessing and interacting with our engineering services, for improved speed, reuse and innovation. Built with co-creation, flexibility and cross-functional expertise.

## Product vision

> Supporting engineers at scale with self-serve access and connecting the dots of our engineering services cross software lifecycle, enabling product teams to move faster and go further, and organisation wide unleashing innovation, reuse and co-creation

## Contribution and co-creation

Developer Portal initiative is adopting inner source practises, being transparent and inclusive in our work.

![Developer portal logical view](assets/governance.png)

_Figure 1 - Responsibility and governance of the portal_

### Roles

- _Maintainer_ - responsible for the core of the portal or a plugin, see [CODEOWNERS.md](https://github.com/ingka-group-digital/developer-portal/blob/main/.github/CODEOWNERS)
- _Contributor_ - contributing not only as code commits, but also improvements suggestions, documentation additions, issue/bug reports and more
- _Sponsor_ - sponsor of the Developer Portal initiative. Final say on things when there is a need

### Plugins

Backstage provides a plugin-based architecture for extending the platform, making it easy for adopters to make use of existing [plugins](https://backstage.io/plugins), and develop INGKA custom integrations.

### Core maintainer team

The _core maintainer team_ is responsible of the core of Backstage, including

- Roadmap of the Developer Portal (vision, OKRs, roadmap items)
- Development of central plugins
- Support (aspiring) contributors (e.g documentation, refinement of new features, automation workflows, PRs)
- Developer Portal working group
- Base integrations (e.g IAM, SMC)
- Backstage upstream sync/contribution
- Managing reported bugs
- Operations and incident management

### Plugin maintainer teams

As Figure 1 suggests, plugins are developed and maintained by its own autonomous _plugin maintainer team_, supported by the core maintainer team, in order to create relevant engineering centric workflows.

### Working group

The working group is for maintainers and contributors, supporting in the co-creation of the Developer Portal. Activities includes weekly syncs, refinement work, early demos and feedback, managing [issues in GitHub](https://github.com/ingka-group-digital/developer-portal/issues), roadmap.

If interested in the working group, join Slack channel [#dt-developer-portal-dev](https://ingka.slack.com/archives/C01VDM1NE0Y)

## Roadmap

The roadmap is governed by the maintainer team, with close collaboration with working group and stakeholders. More on vision, OKRs, and roadmap, is found at https://confluence.build.ingka.ikea.com/display/DevP/Developer+Portal.

## Upstream Backstage project

The developer Portal is based on Backstage open source project, originally developed and adopted as an internal engineering tool at Spotify. Backstage is today a Cloud Native Computing Foundation (CNCF) sandbox project.

Backstage’s ability to simplify tooling and standardize engineering practices has attracted interest and is being adopted by many enterprises with similar challenges, and thus has also created a healthy base of contributors to the project.
